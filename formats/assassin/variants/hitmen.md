# Hitmen

Hitmen is a local variant of the broader Assassin format. Each player receives a secret Mark. Instead of a normal free-for-all, players can only attack or target their current Mark unless the rules below expand their legal targets.

## Terminology

- A **Mark Card** is the numbered card dealt to a player to indicate their assigned target.
- A player's **Mark** is the player indicated by their current Mark Card.
- A player's **Sphere** consists of that player and anything targetable that player controls. This includes permanents, tokens, spells and abilities on the stack, and any other targetable game object a card or rule may care about.

## Setup

- Mark Cards are numbered `1` through `X`, where `X` is the number of players.
- Each player receives one Mark Card.
- Each number corresponds to another player.

## Targeting Rules

- You may only attack your Mark.
- Your spells and abilities may only target your Mark's Sphere.
- If you do not have enough valid targets, you may also target the Spheres of players next to your Mark.
- If you still do not have enough valid targets, continue expanding outward from the previous set of players until the effect can be completed or all players are included.
- When an effect on the stack affects you or anything you control, even if it does not target, you may target that effect or its controller's Sphere until the stack resolves.

## Changing Marks

When your Mark dies, take their Mark Card. That card indicates your new Mark.

If a player ever has their own number as a Mark Card, including during setup, they become a Free Agent and may attack or target anyone.

## Win Condition

Be the last player standing.

As a shorter game option, the table may set a required number of kills instead.
