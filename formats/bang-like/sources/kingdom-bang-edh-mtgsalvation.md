# Kingdom / BANG EDH

Source: <https://www.mtgsalvation.com/forums/the-game/commander-edh/616543-kingdom-aka-bang-edh-a-variant-format>

Original poster: `kesofcom`  
Context: MTGSalvation Commander/EDH forum thread, July 2015.

This source presents a 5-6 player Commander variant inspired by BANG! and Three Kingdoms-style hidden-role games.

## Setup

This is a role-playing variant used with Commander decks. Each role has its own win condition, and some roles have additional rules.

Prepare a role-card set:

| Role | Count |
| --- | ---: |
| Emperor | 1 |
| Guard | 1 |
| Assassin | 2 |
| Rogue | 1 |

For a 6-player game, add one additional Assassin.

Shuffle the role cards and deal one to each player. A player's role does not change during the game.

## Role Visibility

- The Emperor reveals their role.
- All other roles stay secret and face down.
- Roles are revealed only when a player dies or when the game ends.
- Players cannot concede.

## Table-Talk Rules

The source is explicit that players should not use out-of-game leverage to prove their roles.

Players may not make external promises such as "if I am lying, you can tear up my card."

Players also cannot talk about their role directly. They may discuss actions they have taken in the game, such as reminding another player that they destroyed a token army attacking them.

## Role Table

| Role | Win Condition | Bonus / Penalty |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Emperor | The Emperor is alive, all Assassins are dead, and the Rogue is dead. The Guard's status does not affect the Emperor's win. | Starts first, +10 starting life, +1 starting hand size, +1 maximum hand size. |
| Guard | All Assassins are dead, the Rogue is dead, and the Emperor is not dead. The Guard can be dead and still win. | No listed bonus. |
| Assassin | The Emperor is dead, and the Rogue's win condition has not been fulfilled. Any or all Assassins can be dead and still win. | When an Assassin dies, the player who killed them may draw up to three cards. |
| Rogue | The Rogue is alive, all players except the Emperor and Rogue are alive, and the Rogue kills the Emperor. | Alternate condition: the Rogue may kill all other players at once if the Emperor is last or tied for last to die. |

## Emperor Penalty

If the Emperor kills the Guard, the Emperor exiles their nonland permanents, graveyard, and hand.

## Rogue Redeal Rule

If a player was the Rogue in the previous game, they may request a redeal before the next game starts, until they are not the Rogue again.

## Design Notes From The Thread

The discussion focuses heavily on making the Rogue playable and preventing role-proofing.

The poster notes:

- A three-card reward for killing an Assassin is enough incentive without encouraging too much early killing.
- The Emperor needs a meaningful penalty for killing the Guard, or the Emperor can stab blindly.
- The Rogue is intentionally difficult but can be fun because it asks the player to manipulate the table.

Suggested Rogue strategies include:

- Include a way to win in one strong turn, such as a large drain spell.
- Increase spot removal and board wipes so the Rogue can help the Emperor and prolong the game.
- Pretend to be the Guard, help kill the Assassins, then isolate the Emperor for the final duel.

## Alternate Rogue Discussion

The thread discusses an alternate Rogue win condition where the Rogue wins by personally killing two players.

That creates rules complications if the Rogue's second kill is the Emperor, because the Assassin team might also be trying to win at that moment. The thread suggests avoiding simultaneous wins by ruling that:

- The Rogue and Assassins cannot both win if the Rogue's second kill is the Emperor; or
- The Rogue's second kill cannot be the Emperor.

## Takeaways For Game-Night Boxes

This version is more formal than the basic Reddit BANG! variant. It is useful if the box wants:

- Clear anti-role-proofing rules.
- A harsh Emperor penalty.
- A reward for killing Assassins.
- A Rogue role with explicit strategy and balance support.
