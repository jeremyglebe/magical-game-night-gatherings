Cube ​

Cube is a Limited format built from a curated pool of Magic cards. Instead of opening retail booster packs, players draft from custom "packs" made from the cube owner's card pool, then build decks from the cards they drafted. Wizards describes Cube as being like Booster Draft, but with cards pulled from across Magic's history. Source: Arena Powered Cube Draft.

The cube itself is not a deck. It is the reusable draft environment.

Basic Concept ​

A cube is usually a few hundred cards selected by its designer. The designer chooses the themes, power level, colors, archetypes, and constraints. Before a draft, the cube is randomized and divided into draft packs, usually fifteen cards each. Players draft those packs like a normal Booster Draft, build decks, play matches, and return the cards to the cube afterward. Source: Power, Cubed.

Most cubes are singleton, meaning they include only one copy of each non-basic-land card. That is common, but not required. Some cubes use duplicates for balance, theme, or to support specific archetypes. Wizards' cube articles describe singleton as typical for many cubes, while also noting variations in cube size, restriction, and structure. Sources: Power, Cubed, Building Your First Cube.

Why Build A Cube? ​

Cube lets a group draft the kind of Magic environment they want on demand. It can feel like a greatest-hits Limited format, a nostalgia set, a custom expansion, a high-powered Vintage draft, a peasant environment, a set cube, a Commander draft box, or something much stranger.

For game-night purposes, cube is useful because the host can prepare the entire experience in advance:

  • The card pool.
  • The draft structure.
  • The land station.
  • The number of players supported.
  • The intended power level.
  • Any house rules or special draft rules.

Common Cube Types ​

  • Powered Cube: High-powered cards, often including extremely efficient spells and fast mana.
  • Unpowered Cube: Strong cards, but without the most explosive power outliers.
  • Peasant Cube: Only commons and uncommons.
  • Pauper Cube: Only commons.
  • Set Cube: Recreates the feel of drafting a specific Magic set, often with duplicates.
  • Theme Cube: Built around a plane, mechanic, creature type, color restriction, story, or custom design goal.
  • Commander Cube: Drafted for multiplayer Commander-style games, usually with special commander selection rules.

Drafting And Building ​

The default cube experience mirrors Booster Draft:

  • Each player drafts three packs.
  • Packs are often fifteen cards.
  • Players build minimum forty-card decks.
  • Basic lands are supplied separately.

That default is flexible. Smaller groups might draft more packs, use sealed pools, grid draft, Winston draft, rotisserie draft, or other draft variants. A cube can support whatever draft method the owner wants. Wizards' cube guidance emphasizes that cube size and structure are design choices rather than one fixed template. Source: Building Your First Cube.

Boxed-Set Notes ​

A cube box should probably include:

  • The cube card pool.
  • Basic lands or a land station.
  • Draft instructions.
  • Player-count guidance.
  • Any custom rules or archetype notes.
  • Tokens, counters, and helper cards used by the cube.
  • A changelog if the cube evolves over time.
