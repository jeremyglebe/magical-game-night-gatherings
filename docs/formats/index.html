Formats ​

Formats are the rulesets or play structures that can support a game night. Some are broad families, while others are specific variants with their own setup and components.

Current Formats ​

FormatBriefSub variants / notes
BANG-like hidden role formatsSecret-role multiplayer formats built around a public leader, hidden allies, attackers, and solo chaos roles.Kingdoms, BANG EDH / Kingdom-Bang EDH, BANG! Magic, MTG Treachery, and the Feudalism boxed-set concept.
Big DeckShared-library and large communal-pool formats for chaotic, low-prep games.Pile o' Magic is the local formalized version with Heap Lands and Commander options.
Creature FeaturePegasus Loft 60-card constructed format with creature-density and shared creature-type deck-building rules.Event knobs include temporary bans for winning creature types and a ban on negative creature-type hate.
CubeReusable draft environments built from a curated card pool instead of retail boosters.Common forms include powered, unpowered, peasant, pauper, set, theme, and Commander cubes, plus alternate draft methods.
AssassinTarget-assignment formats where each player receives a secret mark or quarry.Hitmen is the local Pegasus Loft variant with constrained attacking and targeting.
ArchenemyOne-villain-versus-the-table formats where the archenemy uses a scheme deck.Standard Archenemy uses 60-card decks; Archenemy Commander turns the structure into a Commander boss fight.
HordeCooperative player team versus an automated enemy deck that casts waves of threats.Tracks Classic Zombie Horde, HordeMagic.com EDH-style rules, Raid-style staged decks, and survivor or companion-deck versions.
