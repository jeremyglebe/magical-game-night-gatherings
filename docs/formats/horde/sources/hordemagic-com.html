HordeMagic.com EDH-Style Variant ​

Source: https://hordemagic.com/

This source presents a modern EDH-style Horde Magic variant inspired by Peter Knudson's original Horde Magic rules.

Related Source Links ​

  • Original Horde Magic article: https://www.quietspeculation.com/2011/09/horde-magic-a-new-way-to-play-magic-and-survive-zombie-invasions/
  • Horde Magic follow-up: https://www.quietspeculation.com/2011/10/horde-magic-an-open-source-project/
  • MTG Wiki rules summary: https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Horde_Magic#Rules_for_The_Horde

Premise ​

Horde Magic is a cooperative Magic format where one to four players fight an automated enemy deck. This version assumes players use EDH decks against a large Horde library.

Player Setup ​

  • Each player chooses an EDH deck.
  • Players take three setup turns before the Horde takes its first turn, unless the scenario says otherwise.
  • The setup turns let players establish a minimal board before the Horde begins producing waves.

Horde Gameplay ​

  • The Horde has a roughly 300-card library.
  • The Horde has infinite mana for casting costs and activation costs.
  • The Horde casts spells in waves.
  • Cards are cast from the top of the Horde library until the wave ends.
  • The Horde does not have a hand.
  • If the Horde would draw a card, it puts that card directly into play instead.
  • If a Horde-owned permanent would return to the Horde's hand, put it on top of the Horde library instead.
  • If the Horde is instructed to discard, it may put the top card of its library into its graveyard.

Creature Rules ​

  • Horde creatures have haste.
  • Horde creatures are goaded unless otherwise stated.
  • Only Horde creatures with defender can block.
  • Card-triggered abilities happen immediately.
  • Activated abilities follow summoning sickness.
  • Activated abilities happen during the post-combat main phase.
  • A permanent with an activated ability can activate that ability only once per turn.

Waves ​

  • The Horde plays one wave per turn by default.
  • Optional: Increase wave count based on the number of players.
  • Optional: Use a repeating wave count pattern such as 1, 2, 3, 2, 1, 2, 3, 2, 1.
  • Waves end when an uncommon, rare, or mythic card enters the battlefield.

Player Team Rules ​

  • One to four human players can battle the Horde.
  • Human players act as a single team, taking turns and phases in unison.
  • The Horde is also treated as a player.
  • Player life totals are combined.

Damage And Life ​

  • Damage dealt to the Horde mills that many cards from the Horde library.
  • Optional recommended rule: If a legendary creature is milled this way, it enters the battlefield and then phases out instead.
  • Lifegain for the Horde has no effect.
  • Optional: Horde lifegain may put cards from the Horde graveyard on the bottom of the Horde library.
  • Damage dealt to the players reduces the shared life total.
  • Lifegain by the players increases the shared life total.

"Each Player" And "Each Opponent" ​

  • Effects that refer to each player trigger for each player.
  • Effects that refer to each opponent trigger only once.

Horde Decisions ​

When the Horde must make a decision, make the decision in the Horde's best interest. The source emphasizes honesty: the challenge is the fun.

Win Conditions ​

The players win if:

  • The Horde library is empty.
  • The Horde battlefield is empty.

The Horde wins if:

  • The players have no life.
  • The players have no cards to draw.
  • The players reach the poison limit.

Cards with alternate win conditions, such as "you win the game" text, are not legal.

Ban Philosophy ​

The ban list is meant to prevent cards that make the Horde nonfunctional or mathematically trivial to beat. Permanents with one-shot effects are more acceptable than static effects that permanently shut down the Horde.

Cards are suspect if they:

  • Make Horde creatures enter tapped.
  • Kill Horde creatures as they enter.
  • Mill the Horde on enter-the-battlefield or graveyard triggers.
  • Gain life based on the cumulative power of attacks.
  • Make the matchup mathematically too easy.

Banned Combos ​

  • Midnight Guard + Elemental Mastery
  • Exquisite Blood + Sanguine Bond
