Horde Magic: A New Way To Play Magic And Survive Zombie Invasions ​

Source: https://www.quietspeculation.com/2011/09/horde-magic-a-new-way-to-play-magic-and-survive-zombie-invasions/

Author: Peter Knudson
Published: September 29, 2011

This is the original Horde Magic article. It introduces the format as a cooperative Magic variant where players team up against an automated Zombie Horde deck.

Premise ​

Horde Magic is framed as "Left 4 Dead" Magic: the players are Survivors trying to outlast a zombie apocalypse.

The Horde has no pilot, no hand, and no life total. It attacks relentlessly. The players win by surviving long enough to exhaust the Horde deck and clear the battlefield.

Basic Setup ​

  • Each Survivor uses a Commander deck, though the article notes that other Magic decks can work.
  • One to four Survivors play cooperatively.
  • Survivors share a life total.
  • The Horde deck starts with no hand and no permanents.
  • Survivors take three setup turns before the Horde takes its first turn.
  • After setup, the Survivors and Horde alternate turns.

The article's method is to build a 100-card Horde deck, then randomly remove cards for smaller player counts.

Survivor Rules ​

  • Survivors act like a team.
  • Survivors take their turns simultaneously.
  • Survivors attack and block as a team.
  • The shared life total is 20 life per player.

Horde Turn ​

On each Horde turn:

  1. Reveal the top card of the Horde library.
  2. If it is a Zombie token, keep revealing cards.
  3. Stop when a non-token card is revealed.
  4. Cast all revealed tokens.
  5. Cast the revealed non-token card last.
  6. Move to combat.

All Zombies have haste and must attack each turn if able.

Damaging The Horde ​

The Horde has no life total. For each point of damage dealt to the Horde, mill one card from the top of the Horde library.

The Survivors win when:

  • The Horde library is empty.
  • The Horde controls no Zombies.

The Horde wins when:

  • The Survivors' shared life total reaches 0.

Rules Notes ​

  • The Horde deck should avoid cards that require decisions.
  • If the Horde must make a choice, make the choice randomly.
  • Horde tokens and spells use the stack and can be responded to.
  • The Horde has infinite mana, so effects like Propaganda and Mana Leak do not function as normal answers.
  • If a permanent is returned to the Horde's hand, it is cast again during the Horde's next main phase.
  • If something does not work cleanly, the table should choose the fairest execution or cycle the problematic card.

Design Notes ​

The article discusses a few early design choices:

  • The deck needs variance, but dice-based variance felt inelegant.
  • Non-token cards create tension because players do not know whether the next reveal is harmless or devastating.
  • Sweepers and reset effects pressure Survivors to attack the Horde instead of turtling forever.
  • Scaling is difficult because every additional Survivor adds more draw steps, mana development, and actions.
  • Persistent effects such as Bad Moon help the Horde scale as the game goes longer.

Future Idea: Survivor Decks ​

The editor's note suggests preconstructed Survivor decks designed and balanced against the Horde. This would make the format more plug-and-play for players without Commander decks and could create class-like roles for the Survivor team.
