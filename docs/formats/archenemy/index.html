Archenemy ​

Archenemy is an official multiplayer Magic format where one player becomes the villain and fights a team of heroes. Wizards describes it as one archenemy player facing three or more players on a team, with the archenemy using both a normal deck and a separate scheme deck. Source: Wizards Archenemy format page.

Core Concept ​

  • One player is the archenemy.
  • The other players form a team against the archenemy.
  • Each player still brings a normal Magic deck.
  • The archenemy also brings a scheme deck.
  • At the beginning of the archenemy's first main phase each turn, they reveal the top scheme and set it in motion.
  • Schemes create big villain moments: extra turns, board pressure, resource swings, punishments for the heroes, or other dramatic effects.

Standard Archenemy ​

In the traditional version, the archenemy uses a normal 60-card-or-larger deck plus a scheme deck of at least twenty oversized scheme cards. The hero team also uses normal 60-card-or-larger decks. Source: Wizards Archenemy format page.

The usual setup is one archenemy against three or more heroes. The archenemy starts with more life than a normal player, goes first, and still draws on their first turn. The heroes each have their own life total, but they coordinate as a team. Source: Wizards Archenemy format page.

Archenemy Commander ​

Archenemy also works naturally as a Commander night format. In Archenemy Commander, the archenemy uses a Commander deck and a scheme deck of at least ten unique oversized scheme cards. The archenemy starts with a higher life total than in regular Archenemy, making the game feel closer to a Commander boss fight. Source: Wizards Archenemy Commander format page.

This is especially useful for boxed game nights because the box can include one villain deck, one scheme deck, and several hero decks designed to fight it.

Why It Works For Game Night ​

  • It gives one player permission to be theatrical and overpowered.
  • It creates an easy cooperative table structure.
  • Scheme cards make the game feel different from normal multiplayer without requiring every player to learn a new deck.
  • A prebuilt box can tune the villain deck, hero decks, and scheme deck together.

Boxed-Set Notes ​

An Archenemy box should probably include:

  • One archenemy deck.
  • One scheme deck.
  • Two to four hero decks.
  • A quick reference for scheme timing.
  • Difficulty notes for changing the number of heroes or swapping schemes.
  • Optional achievement or campaign rules if the box is meant to be replayed.
